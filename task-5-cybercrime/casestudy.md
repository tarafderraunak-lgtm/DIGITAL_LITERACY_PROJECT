# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, especially among students and young users. In this type of fraud, attackers trick users into approving fake payment requests or sharing sensitive details like OTPs and UPI PINs.

A typical scam happens step-by-step as follows: First, the fraudster contacts the victim pretending to be a buyer, seller, or bank official. They send a UPI “collect request” and tell the victim that approving it will result in receiving money. In reality, approving the request deducts money from the victim’s account. Sometimes, they also send fake links or ask for OTPs to gain access.

Students, online shoppers, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are the most common targets. The consequences include financial loss, stress, and difficulty in recovering the money.

This case shows how important it is to understand how digital payment systems work and to always verify before approving any transaction.
